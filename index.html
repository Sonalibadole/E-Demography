WHAT IS THE
CENSUS

Every 10 years, people across the country and in India fill out the Census in order to have an accurate count of all people in the Asia. The Census determines India’s federal funding for important community services that help support our families and fair share of representation in India

The 2021 Census is happening now! You have three ways to complete the Census: online, by phone and by mail. The Census will secure resources that will benefit our communities for the next ten years.

About Us

The decennial Census of India has been conducted 15 times, as of 2011. While it has been undertaken every 10 years, beginning in 1872 under British Viceroy Lord Mayo, the first complete census was taken in 1881. Post 1949, it has been conducted by the Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India under the Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India. All the censuses since 1951 were conducted under the 1948 Census of India Act. The last census was held in 2011, whilst the next will be held in 2021. Historically, there has been a long time between collection of data and dissemination of data.
